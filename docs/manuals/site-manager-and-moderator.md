# Site manager and moderator manual

This manual covers day-to-day site and building operations. Site managers work across their assigned site; moderators work within their assigned building.

## Team management for site managers

### Add or reactivate a moderator

1. Open **Moderation** and find the site team section.
2. Select a building that belongs to your site.
3. Enter the moderator's institutional email and display name.
4. Create/reactivate the account.
5. Send the temporary password through the approved secure channel. It is shown only once.

You cannot assign a moderator outside your site or create a site manager/administrator. If the wrong building is unavailable, contact the project administrator; do not select a substitute building.

### Update, disable, reset, or revoke

Use named accounts only. Disable access promptly when duties end. Reset a password only after verifying identity. Revoke sessions after device loss, suspicious activity, scope changes, or deactivation.

## Create an invitation

1. Open **Moderation**.
2. Select the correct published questionnaire version.
3. Confirm the building.
4. Choose one delivery mode.
5. Enter only the contact information required by that mode.
6. Set assistance and notification options if approved.
7. Review the expiry date.
8. Create the invitation and verify the returned public code/status.

### Delivery modes

| Mode | Use | Contact data |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Email | Approved email invitation | Email required; stored encrypted and shown masked |
| SMS | Approved SMS invitation | E.164 phone required; stored encrypted and shown masked |
| On-site terminal | Respondent uses a managed local device | Terminal assignment required; no email/phone required |
| Paper form | Printed response later entered by staff | No email/phone required |
| Refusal record | Person declined before response collection | No response session or submission is created |

Simulation modes are for local/demo environments only.

## Follow and resend

Invitation statuses show progress such as pending, sent, opened, in progress, submitted, expired, blocked, or cancelled. A status is operational tracking, not a clinical outcome.

Resend only when:

- the invitation is eligible and not submitted;
- the contact channel remains approved;
- the respondent requested or reasonably expects the reminder;
- local reminder-frequency policy allows it.

Never copy the respondent token from browser history or logs into an ordinary message.

## Paper forms

### Print a blank form

1. Select the published questionnaire version.
2. Download the blank PDF generated by the browser.
3. Confirm title, version, pages, options, and public-code location.
4. Print and control copies according to local paper-record policy.

The blank PDF is generated locally and should not contain identity data.

### Enter a completed form

1. Locate the matching `paper_form` invitation by its public code.
2. Select **Enter responses**.
3. Transcribe each answer exactly; do not interpret or improve it.
4. Add a minimal moderator note only when necessary.
5. Compare the screen against the paper a second time.
6. Submit and confirm the locked submission result.
7. Store or destroy the paper according to the approved retention procedure.

Paper submission is final. If transcription is wrong, follow the correction/escalation procedure; do not create an untracked duplicate.

### Record a refusal

Use `refusal_record` only for field-tracking counts. Enter a short non-sensitive reason if approved. A refusal never becomes a questionnaire submission and is excluded from submission-rate calculations.

## Respondent assistance

`technical_help` means helping with the device or navigation without influencing answers. `full_assisted_entry` must be explicitly approved and recorded because staff may see or enter answers.

Never suggest an answer, explain a question beyond approved help text, or ask the respondent to disclose identity in free text.

## Current limitation

Site managers can create buildings inside their assigned site from the connected administration screen. The server verifies the site/organization scope, rejects duplicate codes, and audits the creation. Ask a project administrator to create a new site first when the required site does not yet exist.

## Report immediately

- an invitation or response from another building/site;
- a clear email/phone where only masked contact should appear;
- a respondent link sent to the wrong person;
- a missing/lost paper form or terminal;
- the ability to submit twice or edit a final submission;
- repeated unexplained delivery or autosave failures.
