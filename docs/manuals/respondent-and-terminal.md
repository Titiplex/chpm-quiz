# Respondent and terminal workflows

This manual helps staff support respondents without influencing their answers or exposing access secrets.

## Respondent link workflow

1. The respondent opens the unique link received through an approved channel.
2. The application displays the questionnaire purpose, rights/contact information, estimated duration, and pseudonymization notice.
3. The respondent starts or resumes the questionnaire.
4. Answers autosave while navigating.
5. Contextual help may be opened without changing answers.
6. Final submission locks the response.

The link is confidential. A public code may appear on operational screens, but the complete token in the link must be treated like a password.

## Supporting a respondent

You may:

- help open the approved link;
- explain how to use buttons, keyboard controls, or zoom;
- read the approved instructions when assistance is authorized;
- help recover from a transient technical error without asking for the answer.

You must not:

- choose or recommend answers;
- ask the respondent to reveal a token by email/chat;
- collect names, emails, phone numbers, or exact addresses in free text;
- inspect another respondent's session;
- promise anonymity when the system provides pseudonymization;
- claim that the questionnaire produces a diagnosis.

## Autosave and final submission

Autosave preserves draft answers but does not mean the questionnaire is final. If the network fails, stop repeated clicking, keep the page open when safe, and follow local technical support guidance.

Before final submission, the respondent should review available answers. After submission, the system rejects changes and duplicate submission attempts. Staff must not bypass this lock.

## Managed terminal setup

An authorized project/site/technical administrator creates the terminal for a specific building and receives a launch token once.

1. Prepare a managed device with current browser/security updates.
2. Open the terminal launch link.
3. Verify the displayed terminal label and building.
4. Use kiosk/full-screen controls appropriate to the organization's device policy.
5. Prevent access to browser history, developer tools, password storage, and unrelated applications where device management permits.

Do not print or post the terminal token. Regenerate it after suspected exposure; the old token should stop working.

## Opening a terminal invitation

1. A moderator creates an on-site terminal invitation and assigns it to the device.
2. The terminal lists only eligible invitations by non-identifying operational information.
3. Staff select the correct invitation and confirm the public code.
4. The terminal opens the respondent questionnaire.
5. Staff give the respondent privacy and step away unless approved assistance is required.
6. After submission or abandonment, return the device to the terminal start screen and confirm that the prior answers are not visible.

## Device handover checklist

- Correct terminal label/building is visible.
- No prior respondent page or browser history is visible.
- Screen, keyboard, audio, zoom, and network work.
- The respondent can reach privacy/contact information.
- Staff know whom to contact for technical and privacy incidents.
- The device is physically supervised and collected after use.

## Lost, stolen, or suspicious terminal

1. Stop assigning invitations to the device.
2. Revoke the terminal or regenerate its token from an authorized account.
3. Notify technical operations and the privacy/incident owner.
4. Record when the device was last known safe and which invitations were assigned.
5. Do not remotely investigate respondent answers unless the incident procedure authorizes it.

Follow the [incident response procedure](../production/incident-response.md).
